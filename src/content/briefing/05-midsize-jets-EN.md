## Why a Midsize Jet for Mexico-USA Routes?

Midsize jets occupy the sweet spot between light jets (more affordable but with limited range and cabin space) and heavy jets (greater range but significantly higher costs). For typical distances between Mexican and US urban centers, a midsize jet offers:

- **Sufficient range** to connect most cities nonstop
- **Spacious cabin** with stand-up height (6+ feet) and workspace
- **Cruise speed** of 430-500 knots that significantly reduces flight times
- **Operating costs** more reasonable than large-cabin jets

## Heavy Jets: For Long Routes and Large Groups

When the route is long, the passenger group is larger, or you simply want the highest level of comfort and space, heavy jets are the answer. These aircraft offer spacious cabins, transcontinental range, and the ability to connect Mexico with virtually any city in the United States nonstop.

**Ideal for:** Toluca → New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, San Francisco. The Challenger 601 is one of the aircraft available in Numen Aviation's fleet for international routes, delivering heavy-jet spaciousness at a more accessible cost than newer models.

## Special Considerations for Operations from Toluca

Operating from an airport at 2,660 meters elevation like MMTO Toluca has implications for aircraft performance:

**Takeoff distance.** At higher elevations, the air is less dense and aircraft need more runway to take off. Toluca's 4,200-meter runway eliminates this concern for virtually all midsize and super-midsize aircraft.

**Payload vs. range.** On hot days at high elevation, some aircraft may need to reduce load (passengers or fuel) to meet takeoff limitations. The Hawker, Challenger, and Gulfstream are designed to handle these conditions with minimal restrictions.

**Fuel.** Fuel consumption is slightly higher during takeoff from elevated airports, which can marginally reduce effective range. This should be considered when planning routes at the edge of an aircraft's range.
