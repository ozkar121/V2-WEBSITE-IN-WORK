## What Happens When You Arrive in Mexico by Private Jet?

Unlike commercial flights where passengers stand in long lines at massive terminals, private aviation operates through FBOs (Fixed Base Operators). These are private terminals that handle everything — from fueling and ground handling to customs and immigration clearance — all in an exclusive setting with VIP lounges.

When landing at an airport like MMTO Toluca, the typical process looks like this:

1. **Landing and direct taxi to the FBO.** The aircraft parks right in front of the private terminal — no need to pass through commercial terminals.

2. **Immigration and customs at the FBO.** Immigration and customs agents come directly to the private terminal. At Toluca, customs and immigration services operate full-time for international flights, ensuring efficient clearance regardless of arrival time.

3. **Luggage handling.** Baggage is unloaded directly from the aircraft to the FBO lounge. No conveyor belts, no waiting.

4. **Direct departure.** Once procedures are complete — rarely taking more than 20 minutes — passengers walk directly to their private vehicle or helicopter transfer.

## The APIS System: Updated Requirements

Mexico has recently updated its APIS (Advance Passenger Information System), implementing a two-step process that applies to all private and charter flights:

**First submission:** Passenger information must be submitted at least two hours before departure, for both inbound and outbound flights.

**Second submission:** A confirmation of passengers on board is sent after doors close and before takeoff.

This system allows Mexican authorities to verify passenger information in advance, speeding up the clearance process upon landing.

## Important Rules You Should Know

**Consistency in operation type.** If you enter Mexico as a private non-revenue flight, all legs within Mexico must maintain the same status. Mexican authorities are very strict about this requirement.

**Mandatory stops from the Caribbean and Central America.** Any aircraft entering Mexico from Central America, South America, or the Caribbean must make a technical stop at either Cozumel or Tapachula for security inspection and customs clearance before continuing to their final destination.

**Required passenger documentation:**
- Valid passport
- Multiple Immigration Form (FMM) or visa depending on nationality
- Customs declaration (for items exceeding duty-free allowances)
