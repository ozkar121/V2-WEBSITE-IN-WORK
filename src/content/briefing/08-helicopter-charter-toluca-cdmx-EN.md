## Why move by helicopter in Mexico City

The Valley of Mexico metro area is home to more than 21 million people across over 1,400 km². Traffic is chronic and affects residents and business travelers alike. At 500 metres of altitude, that variable disappears:

- **Time.** Toluca to Santa Fe or Polanco is 15 to 18 minutes by helicopter, versus 45 to 90 minutes by road.
- **Punctuality.** There are no traffic jams in the air. The helicopter arrives exactly when it must, on a fixed schedule.
- **Productivity.** Every hour stuck in traffic is an hour lost. The helicopter gives it back.
- **Discretion and safety.** A controlled, door-to-door environment, away from the exposure of ground transfer.

## VIP transfer Toluca (MMTO) ↔ Mexico City

This is the heart of our helicopter operation. Toluca sits at 2,580 m (8,465 ft), making it a high-altitude airport that requires an additional weight-and-balance calculation; during the fog and rain season it can switch to low-visibility operations, where twin-engine equipment is especially recommended. These are the flight times to the capital's main destinations:

| Helipad | Flight time | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Interlomas | 12 min | Direct access to the western corporate and residential district, next to the Interlomas shopping center. |
| Santa Fe | 15 min | The city's most important financial district, a quarter of an hour from your aircraft in Toluca. |
| Lomas de Chapultepec | 16 min | One of the most exclusive residential and corporate areas in western Mexico City. |
| Polanco / Reforma | 18 min | The business and luxury-hospitality axis along Paseo de la Reforma and the Polanco corridor. |

We also operate **helipad-to-helipad transfers within the city**, ideal for a day with several meetings: Santa Fe → Polanco in 8 minutes (vs. 40–60 by road) or Reforma → Santa Fe in 10 minutes (vs. 30–50 by road).

## Helipads in Mexico City

Mexico City has one of the densest helipad networks in Latin America, with more than 80 active helipads concentrated in Miguel Hidalgo / Polanco, Cuauhtémoc / Reforma and Cuajimalpa / Álvaro Obregón (Santa Fe). We reach the main public and corporate destinations, the latter with prior authorization from building management:

- **Santa Fe.** Elevated helipads atop the financial district's towers, with direct access to the México–Marquesa highway.
- **Polanco.** High-rise helipads over the corridor's corporate towers; ideal for business meetings.
- **Paseo de la Reforma.** Helipads atop the financial-corridor towers, with prior authorization.
- **Interlomas and the western zone.** Helipads for daytime operations and twin-engine aircraft.
